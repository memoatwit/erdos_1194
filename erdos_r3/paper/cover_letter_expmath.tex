\begin{letter}{Editors\\\emph{Experimental Mathematics}}

\opening{Dear Editors,}

I am pleased to submit the manuscript ``Witness-split + window-cardinality refinement for $r_3(N)$: architecture, empirical results, and a structural hard pocket'' for consideration in \emph{Experimental Mathematics}.

The manuscript reports a reproducible computational campaign around the frontier case $r_3(212)$, combining witness-based splitting, OEIS A003002 window-cardinality pruning, recursive refinement, CP-SAT, HiGHS MIP, CDCL/SAT, independently verified DRAT certificates, and a Lean formal-proof-search encoding for the remaining two hard benchmark instances. The paper does not claim to prove $r_3(212)=43$; instead it provides a reproducible architecture, a tiered benchmark release, and a precise empirical characterization of the residual hard pocket. The associated dataset is available at \href{https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20463334}{10.5281/zenodo.20463334}, and the preprint is available as \href{https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04016}{arXiv:2606.04016}.

The work is not under consideration at another journal. The author takes full responsibility for the manuscript, code, computations, and all claims made in the submission.
